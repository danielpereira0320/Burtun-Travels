Nashik



Nashik, situated at a distance of 200 km from Mumbai, is one of the most important cities of Northern Maharashtra. Its beautiful surroundings and cool and pleasant climate have made the city a center of attraction. It is famous for its mythological, social, historical and cultural importance. Temples and ghats on the banks of the river Godavari near Nashik, have made this city a pilgrim destination.


  1. Sula Wines



    Sula Wines was the valley's first commercial winery. It was awarded with a silver medal for its 2012 Sauvignon Blanc as the biggest wine maker in India. The vineyard houses a resort to live amid the winery and a tasting room to sample the varieties of wines produced at the winery. Sula Vineyards is spread over 160 acres of area in Nashik and is a popular destination for tourists.



  2. Someshwar Waterfalls



    The Someshwar Water Fall, also known as the Dudhsagar waterfalls in Nashik are located just 2kms away from the Someshwar temple which is a popular shine for the devotees. Owing to the pleasant beautiful view offered by the mesmerizing location, the Dudhsagar Someshwar Water Fall has become a favorite picnic spot for the people of Nashik. Moreover, the cascading falls with the white-like water flowing down it make it an even enchanting sight. During the monsoon season, the beauty of this waterfall reaches itâ€™s peak with the rich waters flowing at its strongest current.



  3. Pandavleni Caves



    Located about 8 kilometres to the south of, the Pandavleni Caves, also known as Pandu Caves or Nasik Caves are ancient rock-cut caves that are situated on the tableland of the Trivashmi Hills. These caves date back to the period between the 3rd century BC and 2nd century AD. The Pandavleni caves are a group of 24 caves that represent Hinayana Buddhism. The mystic caves also comprise of musical fountains, museums and various outlets of food. Multiple monasteries, shrines, water tanks, pillars, and carvings can also be found inside.



  4. Gangapur Dam



    Gangapur Dam is an earthfill dam on River Godavari, and the longest earthen reservoir of Asia. People come here to spend a peaceful afternoon on the garden by the river, enjoying the blissful, picturesque scenery of the river, straight out of a dream. Migratory birds can be spotted in the evening, and there is a new boating facility to attract more tourists.



  5. Ramkund



    Ramkund is a sacred bathing ghat on the bank of the Godavari river, which becomes the venue to host the spectacular Kumbh Mela. Centrally located, and the focal point of Nashik, Ramkund sees hundreds of Hindu pilgrims arriving daily to bathe, and pray. The ashes of the dead are also immersed here.



  6. Brahmagiri Hill



    The Brahmagiri Hills, also known to be the source for the River Godavari, offers some stunning views of the surrounding landscape which comprises of lush greenery and rolling hills. Most people undertake treks to reach top of the hill. There are a number of temples which also dot the landscape, making Brahmagiri a pilgrimage spot. The hills are quite famous as they have been mentioned in quite a few mythologies.



  7. The Regiment of Artillery Museum



    The Artillery Museum in Nashik is the biggest artillery museum in not just Maharashtra or India but Asia as well. It was relocated to India during the India-Pakistan partition. It is spread across 2 floors and is considered one of the best artillery centers in India for training the pupils of the Indian Army. On display in this museum are a number of vintage and modern weapons that offer a sight to behold to all the visitors. It is located behind the hills of the beautiful Pandavleni caves and on the foothills of the Sahyadri Mountain Range. This beautiful location makes it an even more appealing tourist spot. Bofors guns, cannons, tanks, military agreements, and different paintings and pictures of historical events are also showcased in this museum.

  • Auto Rickshaws and Cabs



    Other than the bus, one can hire an auto rickshaw for travelling short distances within the city. Ola cab service on the mobile application is available in Nashik, one can use that as well.



  • Trains



    There are no local trains available in Nashik but many people staying in Nashik work in Mumbai and travel daily between the two cities. The daily commuters travel by road to cover the distance till Kasara which is around 70 minutes away. From Kasara one can board the Mumbai local train. Kasara is the last destination of Mumbai local train.



  • Buses



    There is a huge network of city buses for moving around Nashik. One can use these buses to commute within the city. Other than this, there are Nashik Darshan buses that are run by the government. These buses are especially run to promote tourism by taking around the tourist spots in and around Nashik. It is advisable to do the bookings for the Nashik Darshan bus in advance to avoid hassle at the end moment.
